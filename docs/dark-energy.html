Dark Energy

Investigating the mysterious force behind the accelerated expansion of the universe — dark energy — using statistical and computational approaches rooted in observational cosmology.

Methodology & Tools

  • Reconstructing the expansion history using Gaussian Processes, Bayesian inference, and information criteria
  • Comparative analysis of ΛCDM and alternative dark energy models
  • Statistical modeling using Supernovae (Pantheon+), BAO, and Cosmic Chronometer datasets
  • Utilizing Python libraries such as GaPP, NumPy, Matplotlib, and emcee

Research Goals

  • Quantify the evolution of the Hubble parameter H(z) and the deceleration parameter q(z)
  • Explore possible deviations from the standard ΛCDM model
  • Constrain dark energy equation-of-state parameter w(z) using model-independent reconstructions

Dark Matter & Baryonic Matter in Galaxy Clusters

  • Conducted research on the linearity of the ratio of dark matter to baryonic matter in galaxy clusters
  • Used observational datasets from the SPARC and HIFLUGCS samples
  • Analyzed scaling relations across diverse clusters to test predictions of the ΛCDM model
  • Contributed to understanding deviations in dark matter halo properties at varying mass scales
  • Hands-on experience with computational astrophysics and numerical simulations
  • Strengthened programming skills in Python and MATLAB for scientific data analysis
